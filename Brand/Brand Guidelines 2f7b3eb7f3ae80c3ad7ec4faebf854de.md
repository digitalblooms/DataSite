### Logo Usage Rules

### ✅ DO:

- Keep the ball and crown icon close to the 'Bloomsbury' name

### ❌ DON'T:

## 📸 Imagery

> Our images tell powerful stories and capture the energy of the Bloomsbury community. They represent the power that football has to change lives – on and off the pitch.
> 

### Image Guidelines

### ✅ DO:

- **Choose photos that show young people smiling, having fun, and feeling empowered**
- **Ensure any images with identifiable faces are children that have provided specific, current consent**
- **Prioritise shots where players wear the Bloomsbury kit or bibs with the logo visible**
- **Highlight the diversity of our community** through our imagery – boys and girls of all ages and backgrounds
- **Use action shots on the pitch** – players showing skill, personality, and teamwork
- **Ensure images are clear and high-resolution**

### ❌ DON'T:

- Avoid blurry or low-quality images
- Don't use photos with non-Bloomsbury players (e.g., opposition)
- No photos where rubbish or clutter is visible on or around the pitch (bags, litter, packaging)

---

## ⭐ Graphic Elements

> Graphic elements are used to add excitement and energy to our images. They are used to highlight a player and bring attention to important pieces of information. They can be recoloured to any of the Bloomsbury brand colours.
> 

### Types of Graphic Elements

**Circles & Squares**

- Emphasise a point of importance such as a key message or QR code

**Stars & Scribbles**

- Bring energy and excitement to imagery or titles, especially when celebrating moments or people

**Lines**

- Act as highlighters under text - drawing attention to key headers or info in a clean, eye-catching way

**Arrows**

- Direct attention often pointing toward key calls to action

---

### Graphic Elements Guidelines

### ✅ DO:

- Use colours that accentuate the design and complement the image colour palette

### ❌ DON'T:

- Don't obstruct or cover the player's face or key features with graphics

## 📄 Assets

> All Bloomsbury assets are designed to be a visual representation of our brand. All assets will reflect our energetic, positive and accessible brand personality.
> 

### Flyers & Posters

**Must include:**

- ✓ A clear title (e.g. programme, event name, or call-to-action)
- ✓ Key details (location, time, age group, etc.)
- ✓ The logo - both ball & crown and 'Bloomsbury' text
- ✓ A strong image of a player - face unobstructed, kit/bib logo visible
- ✓ Information on how to get involved (e.g. a QR code, website URL, "Search Bloomsbury Football" call to action)

---

### Testimonials

**Use real voices from our community** - short, powerful quotes from players, parents, or coaches.

**Pair them with:**

- ✓ A high-quality image of the individual
- ✓ Name, role (e.g. "U12 Player", "Parent", "Coach")
- ✓ Always ensure we have consent to use the quote and image – where necessary, anonymise identifiable details
- ✓ Include partner logo alongside Bloomsbury's where applicable
